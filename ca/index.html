About

I'm a biomedical engineer at the Children's Medical Research Institute in Sydney, specializing in the development of next-generation therapeutics through protein engineering.

My research focuses on three main areas:

  • Antimicrobial Proteins: Designing recombinant host defense peptides to combat drug-resistant bacteria
  • AAV Vectors: Developing improved viral vectors for gene therapy and CAR-T cell generation
  • Functional Inclusion Bodies: Engineering protein aggregates as smart biomaterials for biotechnology

With 16 peer-reviewed publications and 250+ citations, my work bridges computational design and experimental validation to create practical therapeutic solutions.

Research Areas

Antimicrobial Proteins

Engineering recombinant multidomain proteins and host defense peptides with enhanced antimicrobial activity against drug-resistant pathogens. Focus on protein nanoclusters and surface immobilization for antibiofilm applications.

AAV Vector Development

Tailoring capsid-directed evolution technology for improved AAV vectors. Applications in CAR-T cell generation and liver-targeted gene therapy using ex situ perfusion models for preclinical evaluation.

Functional Inclusion Bodies

Developing inclusion bodies as functional biomaterials. Engineering leucine zipper-based aggregation systems and exploring therapeutic applications of protein aggregates in biotechnology.
