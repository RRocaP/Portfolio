Ramon Roca-Pinilla

I develop multidomain antimicrobial proteins and engineer AAV vectors for gene therapy

As a Research Officer at CMRI's Translational Vectorology Unit, I focus on rational design of antimicrobial polypeptides that combine multiple protein domains and the development of improved AAV capsid variants for liver-directed gene therapy.

My approach integrates structure-guided protein engineering with comprehensive preclinical characterization to create solutions for antimicrobial resistance and genetic liver disorders.

What I'm Working On

Multidomain Antimicrobial Proteins

I develop recombinant polypeptides like JAMF1 that combine Œ±-defensin-5, gelsolin, and secretory phospholipase A2 domains with leucine zippers to form functional nanoclusters. These multidomain constructs show broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity and significant antibiofilm effects against resistant strains.

Multidomain Design Inclusion Bodies Antibiofilm Surfaces

AAV Vector Engineering

I work on HSPG de-targeted AAV-LK03 variants using humanized FRG mouse models and human liver ex situ normothermic perfusion systems. This includes characterizing lobular biodistribution patterns and evaluating capsid performance under neutralizing antibody conditions for liver-directed gene therapy.

Background

My research journey has taken me from California to Barcelona to Sydney, consistently focused on engineering protein-based solutions for antimicrobial resistance and gene therapy applications.

Research Philosophy

Since joining CMRI's Translational Vectorology Unit in 2021, I've focused on rationally designed solutions that address real clinical needs. My approach combines systematic protein engineering with comprehensive characterization using advanced techniques like single-nucleus RNA sequencing.

Whether developing multidomain antimicrobial proteins or optimizing AAV capsid variants, I prioritize approaches that can translate from bench to bedside‚Äîcreating therapeutics that work in the complex biological environment of human disease.
